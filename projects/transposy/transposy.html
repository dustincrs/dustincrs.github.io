Simple Music Transposition With Transposy

Recently, I've been getting into making simple music as a hobby. In fact, I even purchased a pocket operator to tinker around with (though it's more for jamming during commutes etc). I won't describe everything about it it all down for you here, but one of the limitations of the pocket operator I have is that it can't play every single note in the chromatic scale. In fact, if I heard correctly, there is only one note it can't play at all, which is Bb.

You can imagine that this would be an inconvenience when making song covers. This inconvenience is made even more annoying because I never learned how to transpose music notes to avoid the Bb. In fact, I once failed my Grade 1 piano and music theory exam (which I am more proud of than ashamed of), and I consider myself musically illiterate.

Not one to be put off by my own limitations, I figured that if I was going to get anywhere with making music on such a limited piece of kit, I would have to be able to transpose notes to avoid the dreaded and inaccessible Bb. I found a random article online that said music transposition is as simple as shifting each note in a melody a certain set number of semitones. Now- I can't actually read musical notation and you can bet I'm not the type to sit down and shift notes one by one anyway, so I figured: if transposition is as easy as this article says it is, why not whip up something in Python to do the transposition for me? Enter: Transposy- my rudimentary yet functional musical transposing utility. I've already used it several times to great effect and I even added a function in there for my specific predicament: avoiding notes! Now I can just tell transposy to avoid Bb, and I suddenly have every transposition that does not have a Bb in it- ready to be played on my pocket operator.
